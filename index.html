Frequently Asked Questions

To donate blood, you must be at least 18 years old, weigh at least 110 pounds, and be in good health. Specific requirements may vary, so please check with your local blood bank.

Generally, you can donate whole blood every 56 days, which is about every 2 months. Platelet and plasma donations can be made more frequently.

During the donation, a healthcare professional will clean your arm, insert a needle, and collect your blood into a sterile bag. The entire process typically takes about 10-15 minutes.

Most people feel fine after donating blood. Some may experience minor side effects like dizziness or lightheadedness. It's important to rest and hydrate after donating.

It depends on the medical condition and its management. Many conditions allow for blood donation. Consult with your doctor or the blood bank staff for specific guidance.
